Retail Billing : 2017-18 Changes (Essential Energy)

Created by Kate Daley, last modified by Hin Cheng on Oct 26, 2017

For smaller customers, the time of use energy acts as a reasonable surrogate for the demand, particularly given the majority of small customers have basic metering.

However Essential Energy have introduced optional demand based tariffs for small customers from 1 July 2017 as meters will progressively change out to interval meters.

BLNT3ALLV Residential TOU_Interval meterPremises wholly used as a private dwelling where consumption does not exceed 160 MWh per year and they have an Interval capable meter.This tariff applies a daily charge ($/day), and Peak, Shoulder and Offpeak energy charges (c/kWh).
BLND1ARSmall Residential - Opt in DemandPremises wholly used as a private dwelling where consumption does not exceed 160 MWh per year and they have an Interval capable meter.This tariff applies a daily charge ($/day), and Peak, Shoulder and Offpeak energy charges (c/kWh). It also applies a Peak and Shoulder Demand ($/kVA/M).

BLNDTRSTransitional Demand Eligible customers will be automatically assigned to this tariff by Essential Energy and it is not available by request. This tariff is for customers that have been identified on an Anytime tariff or Time-of-Use tariff but do not meet the associated eligibility requirements for those tariffs. Applies from 1 July 2017 and will be assigned to customers who would otherwise be worse off from being moved to the applicable Demand tariff at that date.
