Secondary Network tariffs

Secondary tariffs apply to separately metered loads on a controlled load circuit which are controlled or interrupted, such as off-peak hot water. A secondary tariff is only available to a Customer where a primary tariff is also in place.

N50Controlled Load TariffsA Domestic or General Supply tariff applies

Applies to low voltage customers whose total electricity consumption, per financial year, is less than 160MWh. This tariff applies where supply to approved specified appliances is controlled such that supply may not be available between 7:00am and 10:00pm.

This tariff applies a daily supply charge ($/day), and a flat rate for all energy consumption (c/kWh).

N54Controlled Load TariffsA Domestic or General Supply tariff applies

Applies to low voltage customers whose total electricity consumption, per financial year, is less than 160MWh. This tariff applies where supply to approved specified appliances is controlled such that electricity is available for restricted periods not exceeding a total of 17 hours in any period of 24 hours.

This tariff applies a daily supply charge ($/day), and a flat rate for all energy consumption (c/kWh).

Demand:

Code: DEMAND_ENDEAVOUR

A demand charge is applicable to all customers on a Demand TOU Pricing Option in respect of the demand for electricity their connection points place on the distribution system.

A monthly demand charge is payable, based on the highest demand (kVA), which occurred within any half hour interval of that month falling into a time period defined as ‘Peak’.

In order to maintain the same time limits during both Eastern Standard Time (EST) and Daylight Saving Time (DST), billing data is adjusted by shifting the data forward an hour to accommodate for the time shift during DST. This data is aggregated for the purposes of billing the "per kWh" charge, but not for Demand Charge calculations.

Switching Tariffs

Retailers can apply for a change in Pricing Option in accordance with this clause. Endeavour Energy maintains it is the responsibility of the retailer to be aware of the needs of a customer at any time, and apply for a change in network price to Endeavour Energy as the Distribution Network Service Provider (DNSP), in an appropriate, compliant and timely manner.

In order to submit an application for a change in Pricing Option, a retailer must complete electronic form FBS 3000 and email the completed form to:
CommercialTariff.Transfers@endeavourenergy.com.au. 

For published tariffs, Endeavour Energy requires a minimum of 30 days notice, prior to the end of the billing cycle to which the new Network Pricing Option is intended to apply, in order to process the application. Applications requesting a new Site Specific Pricing Option, or a change to an existing Site Specific tariff, must be submitted by 30 September. Pricing for approved applications will take effect on 1 July the following year. If an application for a change to a different Controlled Load Pricing Option is less than 12 months after the effective date of the preceding application, a fee may be payable. Endeavour Energy will not accept any application not filed by a retailer, for example applications from consultants or directly from customers.
