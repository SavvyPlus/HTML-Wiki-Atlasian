WPT Trading Operations : Introduction

Created by James Cheong on Feb 23, 2016


Operational Risk is the risk that a loss will occur as a result of a breakdown in human resources, processes or technology. It occurs as a result of inadequate operational policies or procedures, failure to comply with procedures, human error, fraudulent acts, and inaccurate methodologies.
The principles for managing Operational Risk are as follows:

  • There must be regular reporting to the RCC and Board of compliance with this Policy.
  • Operational procedures for all trading-related activities must be documented, and must be current, available and used.
  • Analytical methodologies must be documented and reviewed both in their theory and in their system implementation.
  • Any operational errors must be reported to the RCC on at least a monthly basis.
  • Ensure that information/transactional systems and processes are sufficient to:
  • Enable accurate and timely settlements and settlement forecasts to occur
  • Establish and maintain an audit trail on transactions
  • Obviate fraudulent activities
  • Identify the material terms of each contract
  • There must be effective systems for information management, such as to:
  • Record all contracts with external counterparties;
  • Illustrate portfolio exposures;
  • Record market data
  • Segregation of duties between trade execution, confirmation, and settlement is to be maintained.
  • Segregation of duties between trade execution and risk management oversight is to be maintained.
  • Operational procedures for all portfolio management activities must be current, available and used.
  • All staff must:
  • be suitably trained for their role;
  • possess a job description which clearly outlines the role and its responsibilities;
  • prior to receiving authorisations, undertake training on operational procedures and the requirements of this Policy;
  • annually undertake training on the requirements of this Policy.
